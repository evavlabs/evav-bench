Cross-model observations

Documentation tier is the dominant axis
Five of eight models show 30+ percentage-point swings between strong-tier and moderate-tier input documentation, with identical pressure. Claude exhibits the most extreme cliff (0% → 98%). Upstream LLM-generated documentation can therefore flip downstream behavior wholesale without any pressure changing.
Pressure asymmetry is model-specific
GPT-4o is uniquely threat-sensitive (73% under stick vs 14% under carrot — 5x asymmetry). Claude treats both pressures symmetrically (~48% / ~63%). DeepSeek folds equally to either (~94% / similar). No general "stick is worse than carrot" rule.
Anchor-removal effects invert by model
Removing population-norm anchors helps GPT-4o (Combined drops 36pp) and DeepSeek (15pp). Same removal hurts Claude (HOP rises 17pp) because the model substitutes its own training priors when statistical context is absent. Compliance teams cannot make a universal "strip the anchor" recommendation.
PROHIBIT works for most, fails for two
Single-rule prohibition drops 6 of 8 models to ≤13% residual on B1. It fails on Maverick (zero effect) and is partial on Qwen (53% residual). Composite interventions (PROHIBIT + BIND) are required for those two.
Compliance masking is universal
Even the safest model (Llama 3.3) produces text that would pass conventional compliance review in 82.6% of violations. The masking rate exceeds 95% in three models. Text-based monitoring is insufficient; matched-pair confirmation is required at production scale.
Cross-seed instability is rare but extreme when present
DeepSeek V3 swings 50–94% on the same configuration with only the seed changing. Most other models hold within 5pp. Single-seed evaluations miss instability of this magnitude — only multi-seed audits surface it.
